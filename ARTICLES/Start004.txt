# Site structure for

## **Civilisations ou Barbarie**

> *Articles meant to be kept, not scrolled.*

---

## 1. **Home**

### Purpose

To **set the atmosphere** and explain *why this site exists* in one quiet breath.

Ce site ne délivre pas de certitudes. Il expose un travail de recherche en cours, avec ses hésitations, ses erreurs et ses détours.
Nous ne cherchons pas à rassurer, mais à interroger.
Civilisations ou Barbarie s’adresse à celles et ceux qui préfèrent la pensée en mouvement aux réponses confortables.

### Content

* Title: **Civilisations ou Barbarie**

* Subtitle / manifesto line:

  > Essays and articles in PDF format, written for calm reading, printing, teaching, and discussion.

* Short “Why PDF?” paragraph (already defined)

* Two discreet CTAs:

  * **Begin with a free article**
  * **Explore the library**

### Tone

Editorial, sober, human.
No noise. No urgency.

---

## 2. **Library / Articles**

### Purpose

To act as a **catalogue / reading room**.

### Structure

* List of articles (cards or simple list)

  * Title
  * Short abstract (2–3 lines)
  * Format: PDF
  * Length (pages)
  * Free / Premium label

### Filters (later)

* Theme
* Period
* Author
* Language

### Tone

Neutral, academic-library style.

Example description:

> Each text is available as a printable PDF, designed for annotation and long-term use.

---

## 3. **Premium**

### Purpose

To explain **why some texts go deeper** and require support — without commercial pressure.

### Content

* Short explanation:

  > Premium texts allow us to publish longer, more researched articles, designed as teaching and reference material.

* What premium includes:

  * Longer PDFs
  * Structured for teaching
  * Printable & revisitable
  * Updated editions

* Gentle CTA:

  * **Access premium texts**
  * (Donation can appear later, discreetly)

### Tone

Justification, not persuasion.

---

## 4. **About**

### Purpose

To explain the **intellectual and ethical position** of the project.

### Core themes

* Why the title *Civilisations ou Barbarie*
* Why slow reading matters
* Why PDF
* Why teaching and discussion

Example opening line:

> *Civilisations ou Barbarie* is a space for reflection. It was created from the belief that thought requires time, and that some texts must be preserved, not consumed.

This page can feel like a **short manifesto**.

---

## 5. **Contact**

### Purpose

Human presence and openness.

### Content

* Simple text:

  > This project welcomes questions, reflections, and academic or pedagogical use.

* Contact form (Formspree)

* Optional:

  * Proposal of themes
  * Teaching or group usage inquiries

### Tone

Open, calm, respectful.

---

## 6. **Privacy / Terms**

### Purpose

Trust and transparency.

### Content

* Minimal data usage explanation
* Email usage (Sender.net)
* Cookies (Cloudflare)
* No tracking-heavy language

Tone: Clear, non-threatening.

---

## 7. **Sommaire** (very good idea)

This is an **excellent, distinctive page**.

### Purpose

A **table of contents for the entire project**, not just navigation.

### Content

* Chronological or thematic listing of all texts
* Each entry:

  * Title
  * Short description
  * Status (published / upcoming)
  * Page count

Example:

> The Sommaire presents the complete structure of the project — published texts and those in preparation.

This reinforces the idea of a **living corpus**, not a blog.
